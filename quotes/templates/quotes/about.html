About Mamoru Oshii

Mamoru Oshii is a famous Japanese director behind numerous philosophically-bent anime, such as Ghost in the Shell, Angel’s Egg, and Urusei Yatsura 2: Beautiful Dreamer. He studied at Tokyo Gakugei University, after which he storyboarded at Taksunoko Productions, and then became an animator at Studio Perrot, the studio that made Urusei Yatsura. His works tend to draw heavily from the Bible and Christianity - such as the rebirth imagery in Angel’s Egg - and tend to rely heavily on surrealism and methodical pacing to communicate their ideas onscreen. Oshii’s work has been a huge influence on numerous Hollywood productions, such as The Matrix and Groundhog Day.
